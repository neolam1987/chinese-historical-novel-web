Era One · Romance of the Three Kingdoms

三國演義

About 1,800 years ago, China split into three kingdoms — Wei (魏), Shu (蜀) and Wu (吳) — all fighting to rule the land. Their heroes and battles became Romance of the Three Kingdoms (三國演義), one of the most famous stories ever written in Chinese.

Era Two · Spring & Autumn · Warring States

春秋戰國

Wind the clock back another 400–800 years! In the Spring and Autumn (春秋) and Warring States (戰國) eras, China was split into dozens of rival states — the age of Confucius (孔子), Sunzi's Art of War (孫子兵法), and the cleverest tricks ever recorded. Each of these stories became a four-character idiom (成語) that people in Hong Kong still say every single day!

Zhao Yun (趙雲)Shu's bravest general The white-armoured warrior who never loses a fight. 長坂坡錦囊妙計
Guan Yu (關羽)The God of Loyalty Magnificent beard, unbreakable promises — still honoured in temples today. 三顧茅廬赤壁
Zhang Fei (張飛)The loudest general alive His battle-shout once stopped an entire army at a bridge. Ask him about it. Loudly. 長坂坡三顧茅廬
Cao Cao (曹操)Leader of Wei (魏) Brilliant, powerful, and a little bit scary — the story's greatest rival. 赤壁草船借箭曹沖稱象
Zhou Yu (周瑜)Wu's dashing commander Super talented, but so jealous of Zhuge Liang! 三戲周瑜赤壁草船借箭
